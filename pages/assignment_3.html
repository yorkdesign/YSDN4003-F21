Assignment 3

For this assignment, students will focus on translating ideas into a meaningful digital experience.

More specifically, you will focus on designing two levels of wireframes - low fidelity and mid-fidelity.

20% 3 week
Evaluation Criteria

Requirements

Low-Fidelity

By leveraging all materials generated from your peers during the Crazy 8â€™s exercise in assignment 2, you will generate a series of low fidelity wireframes either on paper or on tablet (drawing).

The main purpose of this phase is focusing on sketches. Make sure to focus on the big picture of the workflow such as:

  • What's the core goal of the digital project and how does it translate into an interface?
  • Do users need an account?
  • How can people enrol?
  • Is there an onboarding experience available?
  • Is there a notification centre system?
  • What's and how is the main page/interface of the project?
  • What's and how tasks/functions are suggested for users?

Tips

Make sure to collect all papers sketch or digital drawing into a clear location inside your Figma file.

Because you are a fourth-year student, please don't ask me how many sketches you should bring to class next week!

Mid-Fidelity

The mid-fidelity wireframe is a significantly more detailed interface. The biggest difference is the level of representation. At the mid-fidelity level, all wireframes should be in real scale of your device/medium digitally (vectorial), done fully in Figma.

At this stage, mid-fidelity should focus on all design elements inside their layout canvas interface, such as:

  • Navigation (primary/secondary)
  • Layout skeleton
  • Font size structure (display, headline, body, misc., etc.)
  • Button (primary, secondary) Imagery (location) Etc...


Make sure to illustrate an end to end specific digital experience scenario in order to get a sense of your purpose and intention.

Also since this assignment focuses less on the interaction component (interactive prototype), students should leverage the Autoflow Figma plug in for making links between pages statically.

Tips

All mid-fidelity wireframes should be done in black and white. However, adding a gray tone pallet is highly suggested for a stronger hierarchy structure.
